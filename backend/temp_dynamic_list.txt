The echoing laughter faded, leaving only the drip, drip, drip of something viscous and black from the unseen ceiling, each drop a tiny hammer blow against the fragile edifice of her hope.

He offered a single, crimson-stained glove, the silk whispering a promise of partnership, or perhaps, a perfectly crafted noose.

The glyph on the wall pulsed with a light that wasn't light at all, but the absence of it, and she realized with sickening certainty that she was not in the underworld, but *of* it.

The coin landed heads, but both sides were skulls.

The last flicker of the torch revealed not the exit, but a mirror, reflecting back not her face, but the skull beneath.
